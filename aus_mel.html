Part 1 ‚Äì Ocean, Koalas & Shipwreck Coast

Instead of just driving the Great Ocean Road, we walk the best parts of the Great Ocean Walk ‚Äì a 100+ km track through Great Otway National Park.

We‚Äôll use a self-guided operator: they book small lodges and B&Bs, move our bags, and shuttle us to the trailheads. We just carry daypacks, enjoy the scenery, and take photos.

What we‚Äôll see

  • Eucalyptus forests full of koalas: The Cape Otway area is famous for wild koalas sleeping in the manna gums along the track.
  • Wallabies and kangaroos browsing in open heath and at forest edges
  • Coastal cliffs and surf beaches: Station Beach, Aire River mouth, Johanna and others
  • Cape Otway Lighthouse, where guides tell stories of shipwrecks, lighthouse keepers and the ‚ÄúShipwreck Coast‚Äù history
  • Tide pools and rock platforms with sea stars and other intertidal creatures (depending on tide)

A typical day on the Great Ocean Walk

Great Otway National Park has relatively dark skies once you step away from towns; in March, the Southern Cross (Crux) sits high in the southern sky after dark, easy to spot with a simple star chart or app.

  • Breakfast at our B&B, then a shuttle to the trailhead
  • 10‚Äì16 km (6‚Äì10 miles) of walking on good tracks: forest, clifftops, occasional beach sections
  • Picnic lunch at a lookout or beach
  • Mid-afternoon arrival at our next accommodation
  • Hot shower, relaxed dinner, and maybe a short night walk to look for the Southern Cross if skies are clear

Part 3 ‚Äì Vineyards, Valleys & Easy Bike Paths

North-east of Melbourne, the Murray to Mountains Rail Trail is one of Australia‚Äôs premier rail trails: 137 km of sealed path linking Wangaratta, Beechworth, Myrtleford, Bright and more.

We‚Äôll add the newer Great Valley Trail, a 27 km sealed path from Bright to Harrietville, following the Ovens River beneath the Victorian Alps.

We can ride regular bikes or e-bikes, and use a local operator for luggage transfers and shuttles.

What we‚Äôll see

  • Long, car-free bike paths lined with tall river red gums and poplars
  • Views across fields to Mt Buffalo and Mt Feathertop, often with snow-gums and peaks visible in the distance
  • Vineyards and cellar doors right on the trail ‚Äì this region is known for wineries, farm gates, and small producers
  • Rivers and swimming spots where we can cool off after a ride
  • Historic gold-rush towns like Beechworth and Bright, with preserved streetscapes, museums and local history tours

Our likely riding days (flexible)

Evenings in Beechworth and Bright give us time to talk with winemakers, caf√© owners and guides ‚Äì people who love this region and are usually very happy to share local stories and wildlife tips.

Part 4 ‚Äì Stars, Wildlife & a Scenic Flight

Details on the natural wonders and special activities included in the trip.

Southern sky & Southern Cross

On clear nights along the coast and especially in the High Country, we‚Äôll have access to relatively dark skies compared to home. In late summer/early autumn:

  • The Southern Cross is high in the south after dark
  • The Magellanic Clouds (small satellite galaxies) can often be seen as faint patches
  • The Milky Way band arches across the sky away from city lights

We can bring a simple southern-sky star chart or app and do a few short ‚Äústar walks‚Äù after dinner.

Wildlife highlights

Across the trip we have good chances to see:

  • Koalas in the eucalypts around Cape Otway
  • Wallabies and kangaroos in Otway heathlands and High Country paddocks
  • Birdlife: black cockatoos, kookaburras, rosellas, wedge-tailed eagles over the valleys
  • Coastal species like fur seals and seabirds along the Great Ocean Walk, and possibly little penguins if we add an evening penguin-viewing side trip (e.g., near the Twelve Apostles or via a short extra excursion)

Scenic flight over the coast

One day on the Great Ocean Road, we‚Äôll add a short scenic flight in a small plane or helicopter over:

  • The Twelve Apostles and limestone stacks
  • The cliffs and arches of the Shipwreck Coast
  • The surrounding farmland and forest from the air

Local operators near the Twelve Apostles offer 15‚Äì30 minute flights that give a completely different view of the coastline compared to the lookouts on foot.
