
# Southern Stars & Secret Trails

### A Victoria Adventure for Two

Imagine waking up to kookaburras and the sound of surf, walking under eucalyptus trees full of wild koalas, sailing with locals on a sparkling bay, and finishing the trip with easy bike rides through autumn-coloured valleys and vineyards.

This trip is built around the things we both care about:

* Real **national-park walks** and **bike routes**, not just sitting on buses
* **History and zoology**: koalas, wallabies, penguins, shipwrecks and gold-rush towns
* **Stars**: seeing the Southern Cross under dark skies
* Meeting **local guides and hosts** who love their corner of Australia
* Time **on the water** and a **scenic flight** over dramatic coastline

All of it, with just one city hub: **Melbourne**.

---

## Trip Snapshot

**Where:** Victoria, Australia – Melbourne, Great Ocean Road & Great Otway National Park, and the High Country (Beechworth–Bright–Harrietville)

**What we’ll do:**

* 3 days of the **Great Ocean Walk** through Great Otway National Park – cliffs, rainforest, and wild koalas([Parks Victoria][1])
* A day **sailing on Port Phillip** with local crews from a Melbourne yacht club([SYC][2])
* 3 days of **cycling** the Murray to Mountains Rail Trail and Great Valley Trail – sealed trails through vineyards, gum trees and river valleys([Rail Trails Australia][3])
* A **short scenic flight** over the Twelve Apostles and the Shipwreck Coast
* Evenings in **Apollo Bay, Melbourne, Beechworth and Bright** – great food, wine, and local stories
* Night walks to see the **Southern Cross** and the southern sky

---

## Part 1 – Ocean, Koalas & Shipwreck Coast

### Great Ocean Walk (3 days)

**Base:** Apollo Bay & Cape Otway area
**Theme:** Coastal national park, wildlife, history

Instead of just driving the Great Ocean Road, we walk the best parts of the **Great Ocean Walk** – a 100+ km track through **Great Otway National Park**.([Parks Victoria][1])

We’ll use a **self-guided operator**: they book small lodges and B&Bs, move our bags, and shuttle us to the trailheads. We just carry daypacks, enjoy the scenery, and take photos.

#### What we’ll see

* **Eucalyptus forests full of koalas**

  * The Cape Otway area is famous for wild koalas sleeping in the manna gums along the track.([Parks Victoria][1])
* **Wallabies and kangaroos** browsing in open heath and at forest edges
* Coastal **cliffs and surf beaches**: Station Beach, Aire River mouth, Johanna and others([Beyond Wild Places][4])
* **Cape Otway Lighthouse**, where guides tell stories of shipwrecks, lighthouse keepers and the “Shipwreck Coast” history([Parks Victoria][1])
* Tide pools and rock platforms with sea stars and other intertidal creatures (depending on tide)

#### A typical day on the Great Ocean Walk

* Breakfast at our B&B, then a shuttle to the trailhead
* **10–16 km (6–10 miles) of walking** on good tracks: forest, clifftops, occasional beach sections
* Picnic lunch at a lookout or beach
* Mid-afternoon arrival at our next accommodation
* Hot shower, relaxed dinner, and maybe a short **night walk to look for the Southern Cross** if skies are clear

Great Otway National Park has relatively dark skies once you step away from towns; in March, the **Southern Cross (Crux)** sits high in the southern sky after dark, easy to spot with a simple star chart or app.([Beyond Wild Places][4])

## Part 2 – Sailing & Southern Stars

### Melbourne & Port Phillip Bay

**Base:** Melbourne
**Theme:** Local sailing culture, food, and urban nights under southern skies

After the coastal walk, we come back to Melbourne for a change of pace and some city comforts.

#### Sailing day

We’ll spend a day on **Port Phillip Bay**, sailing from a local yacht club such as **Sandringham** or **Royal Brighton**. These clubs run regular keelboat racing and have crew registers where visiting sailors can join local boats.([SYC][2])

On the water we’ll:

* Trim sails and work with a local crew
* Get views back to the **Melbourne skyline** from the bay
* Hear first-hand what it’s like to grow up sailing here and why locals love this coastline

#### Evenings in Melbourne

We’ll give ourselves a couple of proper dinners in the city – Melbourne is known for:

* **Neighbourhood wine bars and bistros**
* A big **Italian and Asian food scene**
* Café culture and laneway bars

This is also where we can do a **short star-walk** on a clear night – even from city parks you can often spot the Southern Cross and bright southern stars, and we’ll get darker skies again in the High Country.

## Part 3 – Vineyards, Valleys & Easy Bike Paths

### Murray to Mountains Rail Trail & Great Valley Trail (3 days)

**Base:** Beechworth, Myrtleford, Bright
**Theme:** High Country landscapes, food and wine, gentle but satisfying riding

North-east of Melbourne, the **Murray to Mountains Rail Trail** is one of Australia’s premier rail trails: 137 km of sealed path linking Wangaratta, Beechworth, Myrtleford, Bright and more.([Rail Trails Australia][3])

We’ll add the newer **Great Valley Trail**, a 27 km sealed path from **Bright to Harrietville**, following the Ovens River beneath the Victorian Alps.([Harrietville][5])

We can ride regular bikes or e-bikes, and use a local operator for luggage transfers and shuttles.

#### What we’ll see

* **Long, car-free bike paths** lined with tall **river red gums and poplars**
* Views across fields to **Mt Buffalo and Mt Feathertop**, often with snow-gums and peaks visible in the distance([Rail Trails Australia][3])
* **Vineyards and cellar doors** right on the trail – this region is known for wineries, farm gates, and small producers([Rail Trails Australia][3])
* Rivers and swimming spots where we can cool off after a ride([Rail Trails Australia][3])
* Historic **gold-rush towns** like **Beechworth** and Bright, with preserved streetscapes, museums and local history tours([Tour De Vines][6])

#### Our likely riding days (flexible)

* **Day 1 – Wangaratta → Milawa → Beechworth (~60 km)**
  Ride through farmland and wine country to Milawa, then climb gradually on the rail trail to Beechworth – a satisfying “we earned dinner” day.([Tour De Vines][6])

* **Day 2 – Beechworth → Myrtleford → Bright (~70 km)**
  Roll downhill to Everton, then follow the main trail through Myrtleford to Bright: gum-tree-lined paths, old stations, and wineries right on the track.([Tour De Vines][6])

* **Day 3 – Bright ↔ Harrietville (Great Valley Trail, 54 km return)**
  A mostly flat, sealed path along the **Ovens River**, under the foothills of the Victorian Alps. We can stop for lunch at a pub in Harrietville, then coast gently back to Bright.([Harrietville][5])

Evenings in Beechworth and Bright give us time to talk with **winemakers, café owners and guides** – people who love this region and are usually very happy to share local stories and wildlife tips.

## Part 4 – Stars, Wildlife & a Scenic Flight

### Southern sky & Southern Cross

On clear nights along the coast and especially in the High Country, we’ll have access to relatively **dark skies** compared to home. In late summer/early autumn:

* The **Southern Cross** is high in the south after dark
* The **Magellanic Clouds** (small satellite galaxies) can often be seen as faint patches
* The Milky Way band arches across the sky away from city lights

We can bring a simple southern-sky star chart or app and do a few short **“star walks”** after dinner.

### Wildlife highlights

Across the trip we have good chances to see:

* **Koalas** in the eucalypts around Cape Otway([Parks Victoria][1])
* **Wallabies and kangaroos** in Otway heathlands and High Country paddocks
* **Birdlife**: black cockatoos, kookaburras, rosellas, wedge-tailed eagles over the valleys([Inspired Adventures][7])
* Coastal species like **fur seals** and seabirds along the Great Ocean Walk, and possibly **little penguins** if we add an evening penguin-viewing side trip (e.g., near the Twelve Apostles or via a short extra excursion)([Inspired Adventures][7])

### Scenic flight over the coast

One day on the Great Ocean Road, we’ll add a **short scenic flight** in a small plane or helicopter over:

* The **Twelve Apostles** and limestone stacks
* The **cliffs and arches** of the Shipwreck Coast
* The surrounding farmland and forest from the air

Local operators near the Twelve Apostles offer 15–30 minute flights that give a completely different view of the coastline compared to the lookouts on foot.([Parks Victoria][1])

## Part 5 – Meeting People & Learning as We Go

Throughout the trip, we’ll build in time to actually talk to people:

* **Lighthouse guides** at Cape Otway sharing shipwreck stories and lighthouse history([Parks Victoria][1])
* **Great Ocean Walk operators and hosts** explaining how they manage the track, wildlife, and weather
* **Winemakers, brewers and café owners** in Beechworth, Myrtleford and Bright, talking about climate, agriculture and life in the High Country([Australian Cycle Tours][8])
* **Sailors on Port Phillip**, who are usually delighted to talk about local winds, sea life and Melbourne’s sailing culture([SYC][2])

We can easily add:

* A small **local history museum** in Beechworth (gold-rush and bushranger history)
* Short **nature talks or guided walks** if available on the dates we’re there (many parks and visitor centres offer these seasonally)

## Why This Trip Fits Us

* It’s **active** but not extreme: daily walks and rides are well within our comfort zone, with options to shorten or lengthen days.
* We’re using **national-park trails and well-known bike routes**, not inventing our own logistics.
* It hits the “wish list”:

  * **Zoology** – koalas, wallabies, birds, marine life
  * **Southern stars** – Southern Cross and dark skies
  * **Local connections** – lighthouse guides, yacht club crews, wine-region hosts
  * **On the water** – a proper sailing day on the bay
  * **In the air** – a scenic flight over dramatic coastline
  * **On foot and on bikes** – Great Ocean Walk, Murray to Mountains and Great Valley Trail

We get a mix of coast, city and mountains, all within one state and one easy hub (Melbourne), with enough structure that the trip feels smooth – but enough freedom that we can stop for koalas, wine tastings or a really good patch of stars whenever we like.

**Night**

## Day 3 – Blanket Bay → Cape Otway (Koalas & Lighthouse)

**Base:** Cape Otway / Aire River area

**Transfers (morning)**

* Operator shuttle from Apollo Bay → **Blanket Bay** trailhead.

**Great Ocean Walk – Day 1**

* **Route:** **Blanket Bay → Cape Otway Lighthouse**
* **Distance:** ~10–12 km, 2.5–4 h, easy-medium.([Walking Maps][4])
* **Highlights:**

  * Dry coastal shrub and forest opening to clifftop views
  * Secluded inlets and beaches
  * Increasing density of **manna gums with wild koalas** as you approach Cape Otway; this stretch is widely recommended as one of the best places in Australia to see koalas in the wild. ([Wikipedia][2])

**Afternoon**

* Explore **Cape Otway Lighthouse** precinct – shipwreck stories, local history, and dramatic views over the Southern Ocean. ([Wikipedia][2])

**Evening**

* Overnight at a lodge / B&B in the Cape Otway or Aire River area arranged by your operator.
* Dinner usually **on-site or nearby** (limited options, but hosts are used to walkers and good at feeding them).
* If skies are clear, short night walk to spot the **Southern Cross** and Milky Way away from town lights.

## Day 4 – Cape Otway → Aire River + Scenic Flight → Melbourne

**Base:** Melbourne

**Morning – Great Ocean Walk Day 2**

* **Route:** **Cape Otway → Aire River**
* **Distance:** ~10 km, 3–4 h, easy-medium.([Beyond Wild Places][5])
* **Highlights:**

  * Heathlands, coastal cliffs and dunes
  * Chance of **wallabies and birdlife** along the track
  * Big ocean views near Aire River mouth

> **Optional extra kilometres:** If you’re feeling strong, you can add Aire River → Castle Cove (~6 km more) and have your shuttle pick you up there instead.([Beyond Wild Places][5])

**Mid / Late Afternoon – Scenic Flight**

* Shuttle or drive to the **Twelve Apostles** / Princetown helipad area.
* Book a **15–20 minute scenic helicopter flight** with an operator such as **12 Apostles Helicopters** for aerial views of the Apostles, Loch Ard Gorge and the Shipwreck Coast.([12 Apostles Helicopters][6])

**Late Afternoon / Evening – Transfer to Melbourne**

* After the flight and a quick walk to the Twelve Apostles lookouts, start the drive back to **Melbourne** (~3.5–4 h).
* Check back into your Melbourne hotel.

**Evening – Celebration Dinner (BOOK)**

* **Etta, Brunswick East**

  * One of Melbourne’s most talked-about neighbourhood restaurants: low-key wine bar vibe, big flavours, and lots of vegetables and seafood. Open Tue–Sat; **book ahead for this night**. ([Etta Dining][1])

## Day 5 – Sailing on Port Phillip + Easy Spin

**Base:** Melbourne

**Morning & Afternoon – On the Water**

* Head to **Sandringham Yacht Club** or **Royal Brighton Yacht Club** for a **crewed sailing day**:

  * Both clubs host active keelboat fleets and have crew registers / visitor programs; contacting them in advance makes it easy to join a local boat and hear stories straight from Melbourne sailors. ([Tripadvisor][7])
* Expect ~3–5 hours on the water including prep and debrief.

**Later Afternoon – Optional Ride**

* If you’re not wiped:

  * Short 10–25 km ride along the **Bay Trail** between Brighton, Sandringham, and St Kilda – flat, paved, sea views the whole way.

**Evening – Casual City Dinner**

* Keep this one flexible in case the day runs long:

  * **Embla (CBD)** – great natural-leaning wines and share plates, easy to enjoy without a big production.
  * Or any nearby bistro/wine bar that catches your eye.

## Day 6 – Melbourne → Wangaratta → Beechworth (Rail Trail Day 1)

**Base:** Beechworth

**Transfers (morning)**

* Train **Melbourne Southern Cross → Wangaratta** on V/Line (approx. 2.5–3 h).([Visit Victoria home][8])
* Meet **rail-trail tour operator** in Wangaratta for:

  * Bike fitting (touring bikes or e-bikes)
  * Luggage transfer to Beechworth

**Ride – Wangaratta → Milawa → Beechworth (~60 km)**

* Follow the **Murray to Mountains Rail Trail**, one of Australia’s premier all-sealed rail trails connecting Wangaratta, Milawa, Myrtleford, Bright and Beechworth.([Rail Trails Australia][9])
* Suggested route:

  * Wangaratta → Milawa (quiet roads / spur) for a cheese or winery stop
  * Milawa → Everton → Beechworth on the rail-trail, with a steady rail gradient climb into Beechworth
* Total: ~60 km, easy-moderate effort, mostly on sealed trail.

**Afternoon / Early Evening – Beechworth**

* Check into accommodation in historic **Beechworth** – beautifully preserved gold-rush town now known for food, wine and history.([The Guardian][10])

**Dinner (BOOK)**

* **The Provenance, Beechworth**

  * Destination fine-dining in a grand old bank building; Japanese-influenced menu using High Country produce; Good Food Guide **two-hat** restaurant as of 2025. Reservations essential, often booked out on weekends. ([Provenance][11])
* **Backup / more casual:**

  * **Bridge Road Brewers** – legendary local brewery and pizzeria in a gold-rush coach house; excellent beer and very good pizza. ([Bridge Road Brewers][12])

## Day 7 – Beechworth → Myrtleford → Bright (Rail Trail Day 2)

**Base:** Bright

**Ride – Big Valley Day (~70–75 km)**

* Roll **Beechworth → Everton** on the rail-trail (fun long descent).([Rail Trails Australia][9])
* Join main M2M route:

  * **Everton → Myrtleford** (~26–30 km): sealed trail through farmland, vineyards, and big gum trees.([Rail Trails Australia][9])
  * **Myrtleford → Bright** (~30–35 km): very scenic valley section with views up to **Mt Buffalo** and other peaks; lots of food/wine detours if desired.([Rail Trails Australia][9])

**Afternoon – Arrive Bright**

* Check into accommodation in **Bright** – classic High Country town famous for autumn colours, cycling, and river swims.

**Dinner (BOOK)**

* **Koji Bird (inside Reed & Co. Distillery)**

  * Contemporary Japanese-inspired izakaya with local spirits and cocktails; widely recommended as *the* restaurant to plan a night around in Bright. Dinner from 5pm; **book a table**. ([reedandcodistillery][13])
* **Backup / casual:**

  * **Tomahawks** – burgers and small plates, walk-in only, fun atmosphere.

## Day 8 – Great Valley Trail: Bright ↔ Harrietville

**Base:** Bright

**Ride – Great Valley Trail (≈54 km return)**

* Follow the **Great Valley Trail** from Bright → Harrietville:

  * 27 km of **sealed shared path** along the Ovens River at the foot of the Victorian Alps.([Harrietville][14])
  * Graded for riders of all ages with moderate fitness; mostly flat/very gentle gradients.
* Lunch options in **Harrietville**:

  * **Harrietville Hotel Motel** or **Snowline Hotel** – pubs right near the trail recommended for lunch and drinks.([Victoria's High Country][15])
* Ride back Harrietville → Bright (mostly very slight downhill).

**Afternoon / Evening**

* Optionally: river swim or short walk around Bright.
* **Dinner:** choose either:

  * Second night at **Koji Bird** if you love it, or
  * Casual: Tomahawks, Bright Brewery, or another easy spot in town.

**Night – Stars**

* From Bright or a short drive outside town, do a dedicated **Southern Cross stargazing session** – the High Country skies are darker than Melbourne’s suburbs, and Crux plus the Magellanic Clouds are often visible on clear autumn nights.
